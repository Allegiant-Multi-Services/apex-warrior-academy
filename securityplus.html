Security+ Certification Overview

The CompTIA Security+ certification validates your baseline skills to perform core security functions and pursue an IT security career. This globally recognized certification covers the latest trends and techniques in risk management, risk mitigation, threat management, and intrusion detection.

üìä Exam Details

  • Exam Code: SY0-701
  • Duration: 90 minutes
  • Questions: Maximum of 90 questions
  • Passing Score: 750 (on a scale of 100-900)
  • Prerequisites: CompTIA Network+ and 2 years of IT experience recommended
  • Validity: 3 years

üéØ Domains Covered

  • Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations (22%)
  • Security Architecture (18%)
  • Security Operations (28%)

    üîê Key Security+ Concepts

    Authentication & Authorization

    Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Something you know (password)
    • Something you have (token)
    • Something you are (biometric)

    Authorization Models:

    • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
    • Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC)
    • Mandatory Access Control (MAC)
    • Discretionary Access Control (DAC)

    Network Security

    Firewall Types:

    • Packet-filtering firewalls
    • Stateful inspection firewalls
    • Application-layer firewalls
    • Next-generation firewalls (NGFW)

    Network Segmentation:

    • VLANs and subnetting
    • DMZ (Demilitarized Zone)
    • Air-gapped networks
    • Zero Trust architecture

    Cryptography

    Symmetric Encryption:

    • AES (Advanced Encryption Standard)
    • DES (Data Encryption Standard)
    • 3DES (Triple DES)
    • Blowfish and Twofish

    Asymmetric Encryption:

    • RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman)
    • ECC (Elliptic Curve Cryptography)
    • DSA (Digital Signature Algorithm)
    • Diffie-Hellman key exchange
